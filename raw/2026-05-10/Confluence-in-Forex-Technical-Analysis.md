# Confluence in Forex Technical Analysis

## What is Confluence Trading?

Confluence trading is a methodology where a trader looks for multiple technical analysis signals that align at or around the same price level. Rather than relying on a single indicator or pattern, confluence trading seeks areas where several factors come together, providing higher-probability trade setups.

The concept is simple: **the more evidence pointing in the same direction, the stronger the trade**.

## Types of Confluence

### 1. Horizontal Level Confluence

When multiple support/resistance tools point to the same price zone:
- Previous swing high/low
- Round numbers (e.g., 30, 50, 100 in stocks; 1.1000, 1.2000 in forex)
- Fibonacci retracement levels
- Pivot points
- Volume profile nodes (POC, VAH, VAL)

**Example**: If a 61.8% Fibonacci retracement level coincides with a previous swing low and a round number, that zone has triple confluence.

### 2. Technical Indicator Confluence

When multiple indicators signal the same direction:
- MACD bullish crossover + RSI bouncing from oversold + Stochastic %K/%D golden cross
- All three momentum oscillators confirming the same reversal

**Important**: Choose indicators from different categories to avoid redundancy.

### 3. Price Action Confluence

When candlestick patterns form at key levels:
- Bullish engulfing at support
- Pin bar at resistance
- Morning star at Fibonacci 61.8% level
- Double bottom at volume POC

Price action confluence is strongest when the pattern forms at a level identified by other analysis methods.

### 4. Timeframe Confluence

When multiple timeframes show the same bias:
- Weekly: Uptrend (higher highs, higher lows)
- Daily: Pullback to support
- 4H: Bullish reversal pattern forming

**Rule**: Higher timeframe trend always takes priority. Trade in the direction of the weekly/daily trend, use lower timeframes for entries.

### 5. Market Structure Confluence

When market structure aligns with other signals:
- Break of structure (BOS) in the trend direction
- Higher timeframe trend alignment
- Order block or supply/demand zone at entry

## Building a Confluence Trading Strategy

### Step-by-Step Framework

**Step 1: Identify the Trend (Weekly + Daily)**
- Use EMA alignment (20 > 50 > 200 for uptrend)
- Higher highs and higher lows for uptrend
- ADX > 25 for trend strength confirmation

**Step 2: Find Key Levels (Daily)**
- Mark previous swing highs and lows
- Draw Fibonacci retracement levels
- Identify volume profile nodes
- Note round numbers

**Step 3: Wait for Pullback (4H + 1H)**
- Price pulls back to a confluence zone
- Multiple levels cluster at the same price area
- The more confluences, the stronger the zone

**Step 4: Confirm Entry Signal (1H or lower)**
- Candlestick pattern (pin bar, engulfing, inside bar breakout)
- Indicator alignment (RSI > 50, MACD bullish crossover)
- Volume confirmation (volume increases on signal candle)

**Step 5: Set Risk Management**
- Stop loss below the confluence zone
- Risk:Reward ratio minimum 1:2
- Position size based on account risk (1-2% per trade)

## Confluence Scoring System

Rate each trade setup on a 1-5 scale:

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Higher timeframe trend alignment | 1 |
| Pullback to key level | 1 |
| Multiple levels at same zone | 1 |
| Price action signal | 1 |
| Indicator confirmation | 1 |

- **5 points**: Excellent trade — maximum position
- **4 points**: Good trade — standard position
- **3 points**: Fair trade — reduced position
- **2 points**: Weak trade — skip or minimal position
- **1 point**: No trade — skip entirely

## Common Confluence Combinations

### Trend Following
1. EMA alignment (trend) + MACD (momentum) + OBV (volume)
2. ADX > 25 (trend strength) + Fibonacci pullback (level) + bullish candle (price action)

### Reversal Trading
1. RSI divergence (momentum) + key support (level) + bullish engulfing (price action)
2. Volume climax (volume) + double bottom (pattern) + Stochastic oversold bounce (indicator)

### Breakout Trading
1. Bollinger squeeze (volatility) + volume surge (volume) + close above resistance (price action)
2. Triangle pattern (pattern) + ADX > 30 (trend strength) + volume confirmation (volume)

## Advanced Confluence: Market Profile + Technical

Combining Market Profile (TPO) with traditional technical analysis:

- **POC (Point of Control)**: Price level with most trading activity — strong support/resistance
- **Value Area**: Where 70% of volume occurred — price tends to stay within or return to value area
- **IB (Initial Balance)**: First hour range — breakout from IB often continues

**Confluence setup**: Price pulls back to POC or value area boundary + technical indicator signal = high-probability trade

## Risk Management with Confluence

### Position Sizing Based on Confluence Score

| Confluence Score | Risk per Trade |
|-----------------|---------------|
| 5 (Excellent) | 2% of account |
| 4 (Good) | 1.5% |
| 3 (Fair) | 1% |
| 2 (Weak) | 0.5% |
| 1 (Skip) | 0% |

### Stop Loss Placement

- Place stop below the entire confluence zone, not just one level
- Use ATR for stop distance: Stop = Entry - (1.5 to 2 × ATR)
- If the confluence zone is wide, consider whether the risk:reward still works

### Take Profit Targets

- First target: Next key level (1:1 minimum)
- Second target: Fibonacci extension 127.2% or 161.8%
- Third target: Opposite confluence zone or weekly level

## Common Mistakes

1. **Forcing confluence**: Trying to make signals fit when they don't
2. **Ignoring the higher timeframe**: Trading against the weekly trend
3. **Over-complicating**: Using 8+ indicators when 3-4 is optimal
4. **Skipping confirmation**: Entering before the signal candle closes
5. **Moving stop to breakeven too early**: Let the trade breathe

## Key Takeaways

- Confluence = multiple signals agree at the same price zone
- More confluences = higher probability trade
- Always trade in the direction of the higher timeframe trend
- Use indicators from different categories (trend, momentum, volume, volatility)
- Score your setups to maintain discipline
- Risk management scales with confluence quality
