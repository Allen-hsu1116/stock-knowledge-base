# Risk Attribution: How to Decompose and Explain the Risk and Return of Your Investments

## 1. Understanding Risk Attribution

### Perspectives on Risk Attribution

#### 1. Asset-Level Attribution
- Asset-level attribution breaks down risk by individual securities or asset classes within a portfolio.
- Understanding how each asset contributes to overall risk helps investors make informed decisions.
- Example: A portfolio with 60% stocks and 40% bonds. The stock portion has higher volatility, so it dominates the portfolio's risk. If the stock market crashes, the overall portfolio will suffer.

#### 2. Factor Attribution
- Factor attribution dissects risk based on underlying factors (e.g., market, size, value, momentum).
- Factors drive returns and risk. Identifying which factors contribute most helps investors adjust their strategy.
- Example: A portfolio's outperformance is due to exposure to the "value" factor. If value stocks fall out of favor, the portfolio may underperform.

#### 3. Sector Attribution
- Sector attribution looks at risk by industry sectors (e.g., technology, healthcare, energy).
- Sector performance can diverge significantly. A tech-heavy portfolio faces different risks than one heavily invested in utilities.
- Example: During a tech bubble, a portfolio loaded with tech stocks may soar but also carry higher risk.

#### 4. Geographic Attribution
- Geographic attribution examines risk by country or region.
- Different countries have unique economic, political, and regulatory risks.
- Example: A portfolio heavily weighted in emerging markets faces currency volatility and geopolitical uncertainties.

#### 5. Security Selection vs. Allocation Attribution
- Security selection attribution dissects risk due to specific security choices.
- Allocation attribution focuses on the impact of asset allocation decisions.
- Example: If a portfolio underperforms, was it due to poor stock selection or an unfavorable asset mix?

## 2. Key Concepts in Risk and Return Analysis

### Risk: The Uncertainty Factor
- Market Risk (Systematic Risk): Affects all investments; macroeconomic factors.
- Specific Risk (Unsystematic Risk): Unique to individual assets; can be diversified away.
- Risk Measures: Standard Deviation, Beta.

### Return: The Reward for Risk
- Total Return: Capital appreciation + income.
- CAGR: Smooths out annual fluctuations.

### Risk-Return Tradeoff
- Efficient Frontier: Portfolios offering highest expected return for a given level of risk.
- Sharpe Ratio: Excess return per unit of risk (using standard deviation).
- Treynor Ratio: Uses beta as the risk measure.

### Diversification and Correlation
- Spreading investments across different asset classes reduces unsystematic risk.
- Negative correlation enhances diversification.

## 3. Asset Class Contributions

Decomposing portfolio risk into asset class contributions:
- Each asset class carries its own level of risk and return potential.
- Diversification Benefits: Evaluate the extent to which diversification is mitigating risk.
- Risk-Return Tradeoff: Assess whether the risk taken in each asset class aligns with expected returns.
- Example: A portfolio of 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% real estate. If stocks contribute 70% to overall risk, bonds 25%, real estate 5%, it indicates stocks have significant impact on portfolio risk profile.

## 4. Identifying Systematic Risks

### Factor Models and Risk Decomposition
- Factor models break down portfolio returns into various factors: market risk (beta), size, value, momentum, industry-specific factors.

### Risk Factors and Their Impact
- Market Risk (Beta): Sensitivity to market movements. Beta of 1 = moves in line with market.
- Size and Value Factors: Small-cap vs large-cap; value vs growth.
- Industry-Specific Factors: Risks related to specific sectors.

### Risk Decomposition Techniques
- Marginal Contribution to Risk (MCTR): Quantifies how much each asset contributes to portfolio's overall risk.
- Risk Budgeting: Allocate risk budgets to different factors based on expected impact.
- Attribution Analysis: Decompose portfolio returns into factor-based components.

## 5. Analyzing Individual Holdings

### Security-Level Risk Attribution
- Asset Allocation vs. Security Selection: Asset allocation determines overall risk profile; security selection fine-tunes risk exposure.
- Risk Factors and Beta: High-beta stocks amplify market fluctuations.
- Specific Risk (Idiosyncratic Risk): Unique to individual securities.
- Factor Models (e.g., Fama-French, Barra): Decompose returns into systematic factors and specific risk.
- Risk Decomposition Techniques: MCTR, Risk Decomposition by Factor, Risk Budgeting.
