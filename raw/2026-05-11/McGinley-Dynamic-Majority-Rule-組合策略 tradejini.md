## McGinley Dynamic Indicator Overview

Developed by John R. McGinley in 1990. Unlike SMA or EMA that use fixed smoothing constants, the McGinley Dynamic automatically adjusts its speed based on market volatility.

The mathematical formula incorporates a smoothing factor that changes based on the relationship between the current price and the previous McGinley Dynamic value. When price moves rapidly, the indicator speeds up to follow. When markets consolidate, it slows down to filter out noise.

When price trades above the McGinley Dynamic, it suggests an uptrend. When price falls below, it indicates a downtrend.

## Majority Rule Indicator

A momentum oscillator measuring "agreement" or "consensus" among different price action components. Oscillates between values indicating bullish consensus (higher readings) and bearish consensus (lower readings). Middle range suggests indecision.

## Three Trading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Trend Following with Consensus Confirmation
- Long: Price above upward-sloping McGinley Dynamic + Majority Rule rises above 50 after pullback
- Short: Price below declining McGinley Dynamic + Majority Rule drops below 50 after bounce
- Exit: When either condition fails

### Strategy 2: Divergence Detection for Reversal Anticipation
- Bearish divergence: Price higher high + Majority Rule lower high → wait for price cross below McGinley Dynamic
- Bullish divergence: Price lower low + Majority Rule higher low → wait for price cross above McGinley Dynamic

### Strategy 3: Extreme Reading Mean Reversion
- Majority Rule > 75: Overbought, consider fading only if price significantly above McGinley Dynamic
- Majority Rule < 25: Oversold
- McGinley Dynamic acts as guardrail — never fight the trend it defines

## Advantages of Combined Approach
1. Reduced false signals — require both trend alignment AND momentum consensus
2. Adaptability — self-adjusting across volatility regimes without parameter optimization
3. Clear entry/exit framework — objective criteria for trade initiation and management
4. Multi-dimensional market view — captures trend dynamics AND momentum consensus
5. Works across timeframes and instruments

## Limitations
1. Lagging nature — still a trend-following tool, will give back some profits before signaling exit
2. Whipsaws in transitional markets — cannot eliminate them entirely
3. Requires patience and discipline — few signals during extended consolidations
4. Parameter sensitivity — Majority Rule effectiveness varies with calculation settings
5. Not a holy grail — unexpected events can invalidate any setup
